The Raccoon Arc: The Week the Strip Grew Up

Plot Point Files · July 2026

In early 1987, barely a year and a half into the run, Watterson did something newspaper gag strips essentially never did: he spent a week on grief, and he didn't blink. Calvin finds a baby raccoon, injured and barely moving. The family takes it in. Calvin is frantic with hope; the adults are gentle and carefully honest. Overnight, it dies.

What makes the arc land isn't the death — it's the precision of everyone's register. Calvin cycles through the actual stages a small child cycles through: bargaining, fury at the unfairness, and a raw philosophical bafflement that the strip refuses to resolve. His parents don't deliver a moral. Mom, uncharacteristically tender, mostly just holds the situation together; Dad admits the truth without dressing it up. And Hobbes — this is the detail everyone remembers — attends the little funeral, and Calvin, wrung out, tells his tiger not to go anywhere. Hobbes promises. If you accept the doll reading of Hobbes, the moment is a boy clutching his toy against a newly discovered fact of the universe. If you accept the tiger reading, it's two friends renegotiating the terms of mortality. The strip, as always, lets both be true at once, and the ambiguity is doing real emotional work here, not just running the usual gag.

Critics regularly rank this as the strip's finest hour, and it's worth noticing why it could exist at all: the daily-strip format meant readers absorbed it the way Calvin does, one day at a time, with a night between installments. A week of mornings spent hoping the raccoon pulls through, alongside a kid learning it might not. No graphic novel pacing can replicate that. It was also a statement of intent — proof, very early, that the strip's fantasy machinery (tigers, transmogrifiers, alien worlds) was never an escape hatch from real feeling. It was the instrument for it.

The arc ran in March 1987 — browse that month in the official GoComics archive. It's collected in The Essential Calvin and Hobbes.
